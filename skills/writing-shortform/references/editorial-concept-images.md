# Editorial Concept Images

Use this reference when a shortform piece needs a companion visual that acts as a visual thesis.

## Purpose

Create a clear conceptual image that expresses the core idea before the reader engages with the text.

## Visual Direction

- minimalist editorial illustration
- simple 3D or object-based metaphors
- rounded geometry
- soft matte materials
- calm professional tone
- slight depth without photorealism
- clean composition with little visual noise

## Composition

- one central concept
- preferably 1 to 3 objects only
- large areas of whitespace
- clear hierarchy
- square format when optimizing for LinkedIn

## Subject Matter

Use simple metaphors for abstract strategic and technological ideas, such as:

- servers or data centres
- maps or jurisdictions
- locks, doors, or gateways
- networks and connections
- devices or infrastructure
- institutions represented as buildings or control surfaces

## Tone

- analytical, not emotional
- calm and authoritative
- suitable for policy, engineering, and leadership audiences

## Avoid

- stock-photo aesthetics
- human faces unless explicitly requested
- busy scenes
- decorative elements that do not support the central thesis
- loud branding or logos unless explicitly required
